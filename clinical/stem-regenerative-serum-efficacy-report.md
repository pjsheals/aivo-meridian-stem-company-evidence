## Efficacy Report)

## Independent Clinical Summary

In an independent 8-week evaluation with twice-daily use, participants reported smoother,
firmer, more radiant skin, and imaging confirmed a statistically significant lightening of age
spots by Week 8. No adverse events were reported.
Study snapshot
An independent testing organization; ALS Beauty and Personal Care, conducted a
prospective, single-arm clinical evaluation under Quality Assurance oversight aligned with
ICH-GCP, 21 CFR Part 50, and the Declaration of Helsinki (see Quality Assurance, p.2).
Participants were healthy adult females applying STEM Regenerative Serum to a cleansed
face, morning and evening, for 8 weeks. Endpoint assessments included blinded clinical
grading, standardized VISIA-CR 5 image analysis, and a sponsor-provided
consumer-perception questionnaire (see Objective/Design/Methods, pp.5, 20–23).
Compliance was monitored by product weight; mean use-rate was 0.77 g/day (twice daily).
All 35 enrolled subjects completed the study; no AEs or SAEs were recorded (pp.5–6, 12–13,
53–54).
Population (p.7, p.30): N=35 women, mean age 62.8 (range 48–70), Fitzpatrick II–VI, mixed
skin types; majority Fitz III and Caucasian.
Perception outcomes (Week 8)
Participants’ perceptions were measured with a validated 5-point agreement scale. At Week
8 (p.12), the large majority of women experienced visible and tactile improvements and
expressed strong purchase intent:
• Skin feel & look: 82.9% “skin felt smooth”; 82.9% “ reported firmer skin”; 82.9% “skin
looked and felt better”; 80.0% “ Improvement in skin’s overall appearance .”

• Radiance & tone: 82.9% “skin appeared more radiant”; 77.1% “saw improved skin”;
74.3% “skin appeared brighter”; 74.3% “skin appeared even-toned.”
• Wrinkle appearance (self-reported): 71.4% “reduction in the depth of existing
wrinkles”; 68.6% “noticed fine lines visibly reduced”; 62.9% “appearance of existing
wrinkles reduced.”
• Pore appearance (self-reported): 68.6% “pores were clean of oil and gunk”; 65.7%
“pores appeared unclogged”; 54.3% “reduction in pore size.”
• Trust & intent: 88.6% “safe to use every day”; 80.0% “would buy,” “would
recommend,” and “would trust this brand.” (All above outcomes statistically
significant vs a 50/50 null by binomial test, p≤0.0019; see Questionnaire Summary,
p.12.)
Why this matters: These results indicate strong product likability, confidence, and
noticeable cosmetic benefits that align directly with consumer purchase behavior,critical
for retail adoption and
LTV.
Clinically measured hyperpigmentation reversal (primary emphasis)
Objective VISIA-CR 5 image analysis quantified features within defined facial masks (p.9):
• Age spots (hyperpigmentation) intensity: Mean intensity increased by +7.07% vs
baseline at Week 8 (p < 0.0001), indicating a lightening of detected age spots;
79.41% of participants showed improvement on this metric. (pp.9–11)
Interpreting VISIA-CR intensity: lower numbers are darker; increases indicate lighter spots.
The improvement was statistically significant and broadly distributed across the cohort,
supporting “clinically measured brightening of age spots in 8 weeks.” (pp.9–11, 14)

Safety & tolerability
Across 8 weeks of twice-daily use, no adverse events and no serious adverse events were
reported; diaries and weights indicate consistent, appropriate use (pp.6, 12–13, 43–44, 54).

Basis: VISIA-CR 5 mean intensity improvement, +7.07%, p<0.0001, with 79% showing
improvement (pp.9–11).

Disclosure & disclaimers

file.”
Long-form disclaimer (for partner decks, retailers,PR)
“This document summarizes top-line findings from an independent cosmetic efficacy
evaluation of STEM Regenerative Serum. The study followed Good Clinical Practice
principles and used blinded clinical grading, VISIA-CR 5 imaging, and a participant

arm and are limited to the population tested (women, 48–70 years). Consumer-perception
outcomes reflect participant self-reports. Image-based age-spot intensity improved
significantly (VISIA-CR 5 mean intensity, p<0.0001). Other endpoints showed favorable
trends at Week 8 and should be described as perceptions or trends rather than clinical
proof. This summary is not the official clinical study report; additional protocol details and
raw tables are available to qualified parties under NDA. Cosmetic products not intended to
diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent disease. “
