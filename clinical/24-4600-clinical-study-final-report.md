## 9 STATISTICAL ANALYSIS

All subjects completing all time points were included in the statistical analyses. Subjects were removed from the
analysis in the case of an AE and/or an SAE (if they are related to the application of the test article), non-compliance,
or Investigator discretion.
Demographic data and Baseline characteristics were summarized for all subjects who were enrolled in the study. For
continuous variables, descriptive statistics including number of subjects (N), mean, median, standard deviation (SD)
values were presented. For categorical variables, the frequency and percentage of each category were provided.
Data obtained from the evaluations for clinical grading were statistically compared to Baseline scores using a
Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test.
Changes from Baseline were considered significant at the P<0.05 level. Mean percent change from Baseline were
reported for all attributes. Additionally, the number and percentage of positive responders (i.e., subjects that showed
an improvement in parameters) were reported for all clinical data. Means and percentage values were reported to
two (0.00) decimal places for all clinical grading parameters. P-values were reported to three (0.000) decimal places
for all parameters.
The significance of the questionnaire responses was determined using a binomial test with an a priori 50/50
distribution assumption. The responses were chosen from the following selections: strongly agree, agree, neither
agree nor disagree, disagree or strongly disagree. The responses were pooled into two categories: the first two
responses (subjects who agreed with the product attribute) were pooled into one category (success); the last two
responses (subjects who did not agree with the product attribute) were pooled into another category (failure).
Additionally, subject responses that neither agreed nor disagreed were divided evenly among the positive and
negative categories (i.e., half of the subject responses will be placed in the success category and half in the failure
category). If there was an uneven distribution of undecided responses, the positive (success) category received the
extra undecided response. Questionnaire data were analyzed with the confidence level set at 95% (P<0.05). The
percent of positive responders were reported (the percentage of the population that selected “strongly agree” or
“agree” for each question). In the report appendices, raw data, descriptive statistics, data frequency tables, and open-
ended responses are listed by subject. Medians, means and percentage values were reported to one decimal place
(0.0) for all questionnaire data.
Photographs obtained were analyzed for the appearance of hyperpigmentation/age spots, pores, and wrinkles using
the Visia-CR 5 software. Data from photography were statistically compared to Baseline scores using a Student’s t-
Test.

Summary of Visia-CR 5 Image Analysis
Images were analyzed using the Visia-CR 5 for the appearance of hyperpigmentation/age spots, pores, and wrinkles.
The following parameters were calculated as follows:
• Count: The number of individual features detected within the region of interest (ROI) mask area. A
decrease in values indicates an improvement.
• Fractional Area: The area of detected features divided by the area of the mask. A decrease in values
indicates an improvement.
• Mean Intensity: The average intensity measurement of the detected features. Values range from 0 to 255,
lower values being darker. An increase in values indicates an improvement.

## 2. OBJECTIVE

The primary objective of this study is to evaluate the effectiveness of a serum when used in a population of 30 healthy,
adult females, between the ages of 35-70 years old.
The study will be conducted over an 8-week period and the following procedures will be performed during the course
of the study at the following time points:
● Clinical Assessments – Baseline, Week 8
● Clinical Photography (Visia CR) – Baseline, Week 8
● Sponsor Provided Questionnaires – Week 8

## 3. QUALITY REVIEW AND VERIFICATION

This study will be conducted in accordance with the spirit of Good Clinical Practice Regulations (21 CFR 50:
Protection of Human Subjects-Informed Consent). These procedures are designed to ensure adherence to the spirit of
Good Clinical Practices (GCPs), as described in:
● International Council for Harmonization (ICH) of Technical Requirements for Pharmaceuticals for
Human Use, ICH Harmonized Tripartite Guidelines for Good Clinical Practice 2023 [ICH E6 (R3)].
● US 21 CFR dealing with clinical studies (including part 50 concerning informed consent).
● Declaration of Helsinki, concerning medical research in humans (Recommendations Guiding
Physicians in Biomedical Research Involving Human Subjects, Helsinki 1964, amended Tokyo 1975,
Venice 1983, Hong Kong 1989, Somerset West 1996, Edinburgh 2000, Washington 2002, Tokyo
2004, Seoul 2008, and Brazil 2013).
The Investigator agrees when signing the protocol to adhere to the instructions and procedures described in it and,
thereby, to adhere to the spirit of GCPs to which it conforms.
The study data and final report will be reviewed and signed by Quality Assurance staff.
The Investigator will permit trial related monitoring, audits and regulatory inspections and will provide copies of,
and direct access to, source data/documents.

## 4. TEST PRODUCT

An adequate amount of test article will be supplied by the Sponsor in containers suitable for use by the testing
facility. Enough test products will be supplied to last for the maximum number of subjects for the entire study
duration. New test articles may be distributed if necessary. Responsibility of the identity, purity, strength, safety,
composition, and stability of the test articles will remain with the Sponsor. Additionally, by signing this protocol the
Sponsor confirms that the test articles being evaluated are composed of common cosmetic ingredients with a known,
favorable, safety profile and do not pose a known health risk when used as intended. The test articles will be stored
in a secured location with the temperature monitored until use.
Upon arrival at ALS the test product will be assigned a unique laboratory code number and entered into a daily log
identifying the lot number, sample description, sponsor, date received, and tests requested.
Test articles will be weighed and the weights recorded prior to distribution, at each visit and upon collection at the
final visit (or earlier, as outlined in the study plan).
Sample will be retained for a minimum of 30 days beyond submission of final report. Sample disposition will be
conducted in compliance with appropriate federal, state and local ordinances.

## 8. PROCEDURE

8.1 Baseline
Subjects will arrive at the testing facility where they will be directed into an environmentally controlled room (22° ±
2°C 40% ± 10% relative humidity). Subjects will equilibrate for at least 15 minutes; the time, temperature and
humidity will be recorded on the subjects’ individual datasheets at the time of entry into the room and again prior to
the start of the evaluations. Should subjects arrive at the testing facility with makeup on, they will be required to
cleanse their face and wait for at least 20 minutes prior to evaluations.
Subjects will be given an informed consent form (ICF) and photographic release form to read and sign. They will
have all of their study related questions answered by the Investigator or designated staff. Subjects who qualify based
on inclusion/exclusion criteria will be enrolled into the study.
Following equilibration, subjects will then undergo clinical grading and have photographs taken using the Visia CR
photo booth (Canfield Scientific, Fairfield, NJ, USA) as described in Section 7.
After photographs, subjects will be given the product (serum) [weighed prior to distribution] and the following use
instructions:
• Twice daily (morning and evening) apply to a cleansed face.
Subjects will be administered a daily diary form to record the times of applications and any product performance
and/or safety-related comments. Subjects will be sent home with the test product to be used throughout the study and
instructed to return after 8 Weeks of use.
8.2 Week 8
Subjects will return to the testing facility after eight (8) weeks (±3 days) of daily test article use where they will be
directed into an environmentally controlled room (22° ± 2°F; 40% ± 10% relative humidity) and equilibrate for at
least 15 minutes. The time, temperature and humidity will be recorded on the subjects’ individual datasheets at the
time of entry into the room and again prior to the start of the evaluations. Should subjects arrive at the testing facility
with makeup on, they will be required to cleanse their face and wait for at least 20 minutes prior to any evaluations.
Following equilibration, clinical evaluations and clinical photography will be performed in the same manner as
described in Section 7. Additionally, all subjects will complete a sponsor provided questionnaire.
All diary forms will be reviewed at as an indication of compliance, weights of the products will be determined prior
to distribution and at the Week 8 time point. An adverse event form will be completed for those subjects who report
safety-related comments (i.e., severe or persistent itching, tingling, burning, stinging) or report such events when being
queried. At the conclusion of the Week 8 visit, the subjects will be dismissed from the study.

## 10. ADVERSE EVENTS

No adverse events are expected from the use of this product. Any events, as long as they are transient (persist for no
more than 30 minutes) or do not result in discontinuation of the product(s) or require any type of medical
intervention. However, any other clinical findings resulting in discontinuation, be important and/or unusual for the
products being tested, will be referred to as an adverse event (AE). Any reactions that are severe and/or persistent
(longer than 30 minutes) in nature will be documented as adverse events.
If an adverse event does occur, it will be recorded and the probable factors that resulted in the condition specified
(see below). Individuals will be referred to the consulting physician for diagnosis and treatment if required. The
Principal Investigator will monitor all adverse events to resolution. Adverse Events will be recorded on a separate
AE reporting form.
An adverse event is defined as any untoward medical occurrence in a patient or clinical investigation subject
administered a test product whether or not it is caused by this treatment. An adverse event can, therefore, be any
unfavorable and unintended sign (including an abnormal laboratory finding, for example), symptom or disease
temporally associated with the use of a test article, whether or not considered related to the test article.
Documentation regarding the adverse event should be made as to the nature, date of onset, end date, severity, and
relation to trial treatment, action(s) taken, and outcome of any sign or symptom observed by the physician, study staff,
or reported by the subject.
The study product relationship for each adverse event should be determined by the consulting physician using these
explanations:
Definitely
Unrelated: Concurrent illness, concurrent medication or other known cause is clearly responsible for the
adverse event.
Unlikely: Based upon available information regarding subject history, disease process, relationship of
adverse event to dosing and test article composition, the relationship between the test products
and adverse event is unlikely.
Possible: Relationship exists when the adverse event follows a reasonable sequence from the time of
administration, but could also have been produced by the subject’s clinical state or by other
drugs administered to the subject.
Probable: Relationship exists when the adverse event follows a reasonable sequence from the time of
test product application, follows a known response pattern of the test products in question, is
confirmed by improvement on stopping the test products and is the most likely of all causes.
Definitely
Related: Relationship exists when the adverse event follows a reasonable sequence from the time of
product administration, follows a known response pattern to the product class, is confirmed
by improvement on stopping the product and no other reasonable cause exists.
Unknown: Insufficient data available to make a reasonable informed assessment regarding the etiology
of the adverse event or any possible association with the test product(s).
Study test article(s) include(s) any test article(s) under evaluation, the reference test article (if any), placebo or any
other test article(s) required by the protocol.
Severity of an adverse event is defined as a qualitative assessment of the degree of intensity of an adverse event as is
determined by the medical consultant (physician) or reported to him/her by the subject. The assessment of severity
of the event will be evaluated according to the following scale:
Mild: Event is noticeable to the subject, but is easily tolerated and does not interfere with the
subject’s daily activities.

## 12. EVALUATION OF RESULTS

All subjects completing all time points will be included in the statistical analysis. Subjects may be removed from the
analysis in the case of an AE and/or an SAE (if they are related to the application of the test article), non-compliance,
or Investigator discretion.
Demographic data and Baseline characteristics will be summarized for all subjects who are enrolled in the study. For
continuous variables, descriptive statistics including number of subjects (N), mean, median, standard deviation (SD)
values will be presented. For categorical variables, the frequency and percentage of each category will be provided.
Data obtained from the evaluations (i.e., clinical grading) will be statistically compared to Baseline scores using a
Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test.
The significance of the questionnaire responses will be determined using a binomial test with an a priori 50/50
distribution assumption. The responses will be chosen from the following selections: strongly agree, agree, neither
agree nor disagree, disagree or strongly disagree. The responses will be pooled into two categories: the first two
